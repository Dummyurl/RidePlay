Contractor Agreement

This Independent Contractor Agreement (“Agreement”) is between RIDEPLAY, Incorporated (“Company”), a California corporation, and you, an individual (“Contractor”), effective immediately.

  1. TERM: The initial term of this Agreement shall be an 18-month period commencing on _the date indicated at registration (“Term”). Upon expiration of the Term, this Agreement will renew for additional 18 months, and it will renew every 18 months thereafter, unless Contractor provides notice to Company to opt out of the renewal. Notice must be received at least 30 days prior to the final day of the Term or any renewal term.

  2. SERVICES TO BE PERFORMED BY CONTRACTOR:

    1. Contractor agrees to perform the following services before, during, and after car rides:

      (i) Grant exclusive use of digital advertising space inside the vehicle while conducting rides for any and all other rideshare services, including but not limited to Uber and Lyft, as outlined in Paragraph 4(a).

      (ii) Attach, lock, plug in, make visible to passengers, and power up the tablet, and activate the digital advertising software and services (“RIDEPLAY”) whenever passengers are in the vehicle.

      (iii) Leave RIDEPLAY on and active for the entirety of each ride.

      (iv) Securely lock the tablet onto the back of the front seat headrest of Contractor’s vehicle.

      (v) Have knowledge of and understand the content of advertisements.

      (vi) Be courteous and help direct passengers’ attention to the advertisements.

      (vii) When passengers express interest in place of business advertised on RIDEPLAY, Contractor may offer to drop passengers at said place of business. These drop-offs must be reported via RIDEPLAY.

      (viii) Remove the tablet at the end of the shift for safekeeping.

    2. Method of Performing Services. In the performance of the work herein contemplated, Contractor is an independent contractor with the authority to control and direct the performance of the details of the work, Company being interested only in the results obtained. However, the work contemplated herein must meet the approval of Company and shall be subject to Company’s general right of inspection.

    3. Time of Performance. Contractor may perform the services under this Agreement at any time suitable to Contractor.

    4. Equipment and Supplies. Contractor, at Contractor's expense, will provide all equipment, tools and supplies necessary to perform the services under this Agreement.

    5. Expenses. Contractor will be responsible for all expenses required for the performance of the services under this Agreement.

    6. Status of Contractor. Contractor enters into this Agreement, and will remain throughout and other insurance, workers’ compensation, training, permits and licenses for himself or herself. Contractor shall obtain or retain automobile insurance for the duration of the Term of this Agreement. Contractor agrees to comply with all federal, state, and municipal laws, rules, and regulations that are now or may in the future become applicable to contractor or contractor’s business, equipment, and personnel engaged in operations covered by this Agreement or accruing out of the performance of such operations.

    7. Payment of Income Taxes. Contractor is responsible for paying when due all income taxes, including estimated taxes, incurred as a result of the compensation paid by Company to Contractor for services under this Agreement. This includes income, Social Security, Medicare and self-employment taxes. Contractor will also pay all unemployment contributions related to the performance of services under this Agreement. Contractor will reimburse Company if Company is required to pay such taxes or unemployment contributions. Company will not (i) withhold Social Security and Medicare taxes from Contractor’s payments or make such tax payment on Contractor’s behalf; and (ii) make state or federal unemployment contributions on Contractor’s behalf, or withhold state or federal income tax from Contractor’s payments.

  3. AD PLACEMENT COMPENSATION

    1. Monthly. In consideration for the services to be performed by Contractor, Company agrees to pay Contractor in accordance with the current ad placement compensation scale at https://www.rideplay.tv/partners/#driverterms, and incorporated herein. Any compensation due is payable on the last business day of the month. The ad placement compensation scale is based only on hours and miles driven with RIDEPLAY turned on and active, with passengers in the vehicle, and is contingent upon successful completion of the services outlined in Paragraph 2(a). The ad placement compensation scale is subject to change and Contractor will be notified of any such modifications by email, text message, or otherwise through RIDEPLAY or Contractor’s RIDEPLAY account. Contractor will be required to agree to new ad placement compensation scale terms via RIDEPLAY before they can earn and receive any compensation for services during the time period for which the new ad placement compensation scale terms are in effect.

    2. Company’s Right to Audit Contractors. Company has the right and capability, via global positioning system (GPS) and gyroscope, to monitor the location of Contractor’s vehicle and the position of the tablet while RIDEPLAY is on and active. If Company suspects that Contractor inflated hours or mileage driven with RIDEPLAY on and active, then Company reserves the right to inspect driving reports from the Contractor’s respective rideshare services. In the event Company determines Contractor inflated hours or mileage, then Contractor may be required to return or forfeit Contractor’s past or future compensation relative to the inflation.

  4. OBLIGATIONS OF CONTRACTOR

    1. Exclusivity. Contractor and Company agree that during the Term and any renewal terms under this Agreement, Contractor shall provide digital advertising space in Contractor’s vehicle exclusively for Company and Company’s advertisers, and no other advertiser nor entity. In the event that the Agreement is terminated, exclusivity shall apply to the entire Term of this Agreement. Additionally, if Contractor opts out of the renewal of this Agreement, for the duration of the six-month period commencing immediately after the Term of this Agreement, Contractor agrees not to display any digital advertising in their vehicle. Contractor hereby gives Company the exclusive right to advertise on displayed tablets within Contractor’s vehicle in any digital media formats now known or hereafter invented.

    2. Other Companies. Contractor retains the right to perform services for other companies, provided those services do not conflict with this Agreement.

  5. OBLIGATIONS OF COMPANY

    1. Cooperation of Company. Company agrees to comply with all reasonable requests of Contractor and to provide Contractor with access to all documents reasonably necessary to the performance of Contractor’s duties under this Agreement.

    2. Assignment. Company may assign this Agreement, in whole or in part, at any time. Contractor may not assign, transfer, or sublicense this Agreement or any or all of Contractor’s rights or obligations under this Agreement without Company’s express prior written consent.

  6. TERMINATION: If either party defaults in the performance of this Agreement or materially breaches any of its provisions, the non-breaching party may terminate this Agreement by giving email notification to the breaching party. Termination will take effect immediately on receipt of notice by the breaching party. Notwithstanding the foregoing, Company may suspend or terminate this Agreement at any time, for any reason.

    1. Company’s Intellectual Property.Company’s intellectual property, including RIDEPLAY, is protected by copyright, trademark, and other laws of the United States and foreign countries. Except as expressly provided in this Agreement, Company exclusively owns all right, title, and interest in and to the technology, software, and artwork for RIDEPLAY, including all associated intellectual property rights.

    2. Feedback. Contractor acknowledges and agrees that any ideas, techniques, comments, or suggestions Contractor may develop or provide ("Feedback") regarding RIDEPLAY is given voluntarily and, even if designated as confidential, will not create any confidentiality obligation for Company and Company will be free to use, disclose, reproduce, license, distribute, and otherwise exploit such Feedback as Company sees fit, entirely without obligation or restriction of any kind on account of intellectual property rights or otherwise.

  8. NON-DISCLOSURE & CONFIDENTIALITY: At no time, for any purpose, shall Contractor disclose or utilize sensitive client data, client contact information, proprietary information, ideas, methods, concepts or data of the Company. Contractor shall keep all of the Company’s information, customer list, and business methods confidential. This nondisclosure obligation shall survive the termination of this Agreement.

  9. NON-CIRCUMVENT: Contractor agrees to use Company’s resources, proprietary information, or trade secrets only for the duration of the Term of this Agreement. Contractor may not use Company’s resources, proprietary information, or trade secrets for any purposes beyond those explicitly agreed upon in this Agreement without the written consent of Company.

  10. NOTICES: All notices required under this Agreement must be effectuated by personal delivery in writing or by email, mail, certified mail, or by overnight courier. Mailed notices must be addressed to the parties at the addresses appearing in the signature line of this Agreement, or to a new address that a party designates in writing and provides to the other party.

LIMITATION OF LIABILITY: COMPANY’S TOTAL LIABILITY TO CONTRACTOR AND PASSENGERS FROM ALL CAUSES OF ACTION AND UNDER ALL THEORIES OF LIABILITY WILL BE LIMITED TO CONTRACTOR’S COMPENSATION FOR THE MONTH FROM WHICH THE CLAIM AROSE. COMPANY WILL NOT BE LIABLE TO CONTRACTOR OR PASSENGER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING WITHOUT LIMITATION, LOSS OF PROFITS, DATA, USE, GOODWILL, OR OTHER INTANGIBLE LOSSES, RESULTING FROM PERSONAL INJURY, DEATH, AND/OR PROPERTY DAMAGE ARISING FROM (I) RIDEPLAY MALFUNCTION; (II) TABLET DISLODGEMENT; (III) ANY SOUNDS GENERATED BY RIDEPLAY; OR (IV) DISTRACTIONS, INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO MISSILE HAZARDS, ACCIDENTS, CONSTRUCTION, AND INCLEMENT WEATHER.

  12. INDEMNITY: Contractor shall indemnify and hold Company and other contractors of Company harmless from and against any costs, damages, liabilities, and expenses (including attorneys’ fees) Company incurs in relation to, arising from, or for the purpose of avoiding any claim or demand from a third party relating to (i) Contractor’s installation and use of the tablet on which RIDEPLAY displays advertisements; (ii) Contractor’s misuse of the tablet or RIDEPLAY; (iii) Contractor’s interaction with other people in or outside the vehicle; (iv) Contractor’s interaction with RIDEPLAY and any other device. Contractor’s liability includes, but is not limited to, any injuries, losses, or damages of any kind arising in connection with or as a result of the use of RIDEPLAY. Company retains the right to employ Company’s own counsel. Contractor remains solely responsible for Company’s defense and must obtain Company’s written consent to a settlement. Contractor agrees to notify Company of any pending suit claiming Contractor has violated a third party’s rights. Company requires that Contractor confirm Contractor’s indemnification of Company in case of a lawsuit; failure to do so may be considered a breach of this Agreement.

  13. DISPUTE RESOLUTION, ARBITRATION, AND CHOICE OF LAW: If a dispute arises between the Parties, related to this agreement or the breach thereof, and if the dispute cannot be settled through direct discussions, the Parties agree in good faith to try to settle the dispute by mediation administered by the American Arbitration Association under its Commercial Mediation Procedures before resorting to arbitration. The Parties further agree that any unresolved controversy or claim related to this agreement, or breach thereof, shall be settled by binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association in accordance with its Commercial Arbitration Rules and Judgment on the award rendered by the arbitrator(s) may be entered in any court having jurisdiction therefore. Parties agree that judgements, agreements, and decisions resulting from arbitration shall be binding.

    1. Claims can be heard by a single arbitrator, who has expertise in licensing and contracts. Within 15 days after the commencement of arbitration, the parties shall come together to pick a single arbitrator. If the parties fail to agree upon a single arbitrator, the American Arbitration Association will pick a single arbitrator who has expertise in licensing and contracts.

    2. The place of arbitration shall be San Diego, California, and the arbitration shall be governed by the laws of the State of California. Hearings will take place pursuant to the standard procedures of the Commercial Arbitration Rules that contemplate in person hearings.

    3. Each party will, upon written request of the other party, promptly provide the other with copies of all relevant documents related to the dispute, controversy, or breach thereof. There shall be no other discovery allowed.

    4. The arbitrator(s) will have no authority to award punitive or other damages not measured by the prevailing party or other damages not measured by the prevailing statute. Each party shall bear its own costs and expenses and an equal share of the arbitrators' and administrative fees of arbitration.

    5. The parties agree that failure or refusal of a party to pay its required share of the deposits for arbitrator compensation or administrative charges shall constitute a waiver by that party to present evidence or cross-examine witness. In such event, the other party shall be required to present evidence and legal argument as the arbitrator(s) may require for the making of an award. Such waiver shall not allow for a default judgment against the non-paying party in the absence of evidence presented as provided for above.

    6. Except as may be required by law, neither a party nor an arbitrator may disclose the existence, content, or results of any arbitration hereunder without the prior written consent of both parties

  14. MODIFICATION: This Agreement may be modified only by a writing signed by authorized representatives of both parties.

  15. WAIVER: If one party waives any term or provision of this Agreement at any time, that waiver will only be effective for the specific instance and specific purpose for which the waiver was given. If either party fails to exercise or delays exercising any of its rights or remedies under this Agreement, that party retains the right to enforce that term or provision at a later time.

  16. SEVERABILITY: If any provision of this Agreement is held to be void, invalid, illegal or unenforceable pursuant to applicable law including, but not limited to, the warranty disclaimers and liability limitations set forth above, such provision shall be severed from this Agreement and the remainder of this Agreement shall continue in full force and effect.

  17. ENTIRE AGREEMENT. This Agreement, together with the ad placement compensation scale at https://www.rideplay.tv/partners/#driverterms, which is incorporated herein, is the entire agreement between the parties. It replaces and supersedes any prior agreements between the parties, whether oral or written.
